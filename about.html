About Me

Luminescent lights flickering within me—for my community, for the injustices that surround us: unhoused neighbors, families in poverty, hardworking immigrants, and unsheltered animals. I once froze in fear and self-doubt, but these experiences pushed me to act. This reflection led me to found Project Mosaic, a nonprofit supporting these communities.

Technology and innovation have always fascinated me, yet I’ve seen them misused as tools of surveillance, exploitation, or environmental harm. I aspire to use STEM ethically, creating solutions that serve rather than oppress: accessible shelters, helpful apps, or energy solutions for marginalized populations.

Projects like ImmigrationAlly, an in-process app making legal resources accessible across language and economic barriers, and my Lumiere research on Guatemalan deportees demonstrated resilience, iterative learning, and problem-solving at the intersection of law, policy, and engineering.

Education

I am currently pursuing an A.S. in Administrative Justice as a dual-enrollement high school student. This degree commences an informative journey, experimenting with policy and advocacy found in law duties. My future plans consist of attending univeristy with a Bachelor’s in Electrical Engineering and a minor in Psychology. Math and engineering excite me because they allow me to design systems—like housing technologies or data solutions—that directly improve access and equity in underserved communities.

Career Goal

I aim to become a Patent Attorney, combining engineering expertise with legal skills to ensure that technological innovations are ethical, equitable, and accessible—especially for inventors from underrepresented backgrounds.

Mission

My mission is to connect technology, law, and ethics to address systemic inequities. By leveraging STEM and legal advocacy, I aim to empower communities, amplify marginalized voices, and ensure innovation serves humanity rather than exploitation.
